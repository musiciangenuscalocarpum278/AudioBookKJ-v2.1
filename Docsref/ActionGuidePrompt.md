# ACTION DESCRIPTION GUIDE FOR AI VIDEO

## How To Describe Weight, Force, Impact, And Combat Motion Correctly

---

# PURPOSE

This guide teaches AI how to understand:

* weight
* force
* inertia
* timing
* impact
* acceleration
* recovery
* physicality

during action scenes.

Without these descriptions, action often feels:

* floaty
* soft
* weightless
* random
* unrealistic

Good action description is not only:

> WHAT happens

But also:

> HOW the motion behaves physically.

---

# 1. ANTICIPATION

## Purpose

Creates preparation before movement.

Without anticipation:

* attacks feel fake
* motion feels robotic
* impacts feel weak

---

## Weak Example

```text id="s4p0m7"
The warrior attacks.
```

---

## Strong Example

```text id="z9u7kl"
The warrior lowers his stance slightly before attacking.
```

---

## Useful Words

| Word             | Effect             |
| ---------------- | ------------------ |
| brief pause      | short anticipation |
| hesitation       | tension            |
| buildup          | charging force     |
| gathers strength | preparing energy   |
| lowers stance    | weight preparation |
| tightens grip    | combat readiness   |
| shifts weight    | physical realism   |

---

# 2. ACCELERATION

## Purpose

Describes how force begins moving.

Good acceleration creates:

* speed
* explosiveness
* momentum

---

## Weak Example

```text id="j4n8wx"
He runs forward quickly.
```

---

## Strong Example

```text id="2q5trp"
He launches forward with explosive acceleration.
```

---

## Useful Words

| Word                | Effect               |
| ------------------- | -------------------- |
| explosive           | sudden violent speed |
| sudden acceleration | instant burst        |
| violent momentum    | heavy movement       |
| lunges forward      | aggressive motion    |
| bursts forward      | powerful initiation  |
| rapid dash          | anime-style speed    |
| surges forward      | forceful motion      |

---

# 3. IMPACT

## Purpose

Makes attacks feel heavy.

Without impact:

* combat feels fake
* attacks feel soft

---

## Weak Example

```text id="2m5cbw"
The sword hits the shield.
```

---

## Strong Example

```text id="s6b4xt"
The strike lands with a heavy impact that shakes the battlefield.
```

---

## Useful Words

| Word              | Effect                |
| ----------------- | --------------------- |
| heavy impact      | strong collision      |
| violent collision | aggressive force      |
| crushing force    | overwhelming strength |
| earth-shaking     | massive impact        |
| devastating       | destructive power     |
| brutal            | savage hit            |
| thunderous        | explosive sound/force |

---

# 4. HITSTOP

## Purpose

Creates a brief frozen moment during impact.

This is critical for:

* anime combat
* cinematic timing
* perceived force

---

## Weak Example

```text id="h4p7zr"
The attacks collide.
```

---

## Strong Example

```text id="1y0c2k"
A brief frozen impact frame emphasizes the collision.
```

---

## Useful Words

| Word             | Effect              |
| ---------------- | ------------------- |
| frozen moment    | cinematic pause     |
| brief silence    | tension             |
| hit-stop         | anime impact timing |
| impact frame     | stylized collision  |
| suspended motion | dramatic emphasis   |

---

# 5. FOLLOW-THROUGH

## Purpose

Motion should continue naturally after impact.

Without follow-through:

* movement feels artificial
* characters stop unnaturally

---

## Weak Example

```text id="z2e8gw"
The sword stops immediately after striking.
```

---

## Strong Example

```text id="0z4u9v"
The blade continues naturally past the target from momentum.
```

---

## Useful Words

| Word                | Effect            |
| ------------------- | ----------------- |
| momentum continues  | realistic inertia |
| follows through     | natural motion    |
| dragged by force    | heavy movement    |
| overshoots slightly | realism           |
| carried forward     | inertia           |

---

# 6. RECOVERY

## Purpose

Characters regain balance after movement.

Without recovery:

* action feels floaty
* combat lacks realism

---

## Weak Example

```text id="5z0fpa"
The warrior stands normally afterward.
```

---

## Strong Example

```text id="w0n3qf"
The warrior slides backward slightly before regaining balance.
```

---

## Useful Words

| Word              | Effect            |
| ----------------- | ----------------- |
| regains balance   | physical realism  |
| slides backward   | recoil            |
| staggers slightly | instability       |
| stabilizes stance | recovery          |
| catches footing   | grounded movement |

---

# 7. WEIGHT TRANSFER

## Purpose

Makes movement feel physically grounded.

This is one of the most important elements.

---

## Weak Example

```text id="s9o3xc"
He swings the weapon.
```

---

## Strong Example

```text id="4k7xpu"
He shifts his weight onto the front foot before swinging.
```

---

## Useful Words

| Word                     | Effect            |
| ------------------------ | ----------------- |
| shifts weight            | realism           |
| lowers center of gravity | grounded stance   |
| plants feet firmly       | stability         |
| braces himself           | force preparation |
| leans forward            | aggression        |
| pivots sharply           | directional force |

---

# 8. ENVIRONMENTAL REACTION

## Purpose

The world should react to force.

Without environmental reaction:

* attacks feel weak
* power feels fake

---

## Weak Example

```text id="n7k2sv"
A powerful attack happens.
```

---

## Strong Example

```text id="o8j4qr"
The ground cracks beneath the impact as dust erupts outward.
```

---

## Useful Words

| Word                | Effect             |
| ------------------- | ------------------ |
| ground cracks       | strong impact      |
| debris erupts       | explosive force    |
| dust bursts outward | pressure           |
| air distorts        | energy intensity   |
| shockwave expands   | large-scale power  |
| clouds split apart  | massive force      |
| rocks shatter       | destructive impact |

---

# 9. CAMERA REACTION

## Purpose

The camera should feel affected by force.

This greatly increases cinematic realism.

---

## Weak Example

```text id="1r4pt9"
The camera follows the fight.
```

---

## Strong Example

```text id="0m6syr"
The camera shakes violently from the impact.
```

---

## Useful Words

| Word             | Effect          |
| ---------------- | --------------- |
| whip-pan         | speed           |
| camera shake     | force           |
| sudden zoom      | impact emphasis |
| handheld motion  | realism         |
| violent tracking | intensity       |
| orbiting camera  | cinematic scale |

---

# 10. SECONDARY MOTION

## Purpose

Small surrounding movements create realism.

---

## Useful Elements

| Element           | Effect        |
| ----------------- | ------------- |
| flowing robes     | wind reaction |
| whipping hair     | speed         |
| flying debris     | impact        |
| sparks scattering | energy        |
| dust swirling     | atmosphere    |
| energy trails     | momentum      |

---

# 11. DELAYED REACTION

## Purpose

Force should not happen instantly.

Good action often uses:

* hit first
* effect afterward

---

## Weak Example

```text id="6q1xep"
The explosion happens instantly.
```

---

## Strong Example

```text id="1n8jtm"
The strike lands.

Half a second later,
a shockwave tears through the battlefield.
```

---

# 12. COMBAT RHYTHM

## Purpose

Action needs pacing.

Good combat usually follows:

```text id="x6y8rv"
1. anticipation
2. acceleration
3. impact
4. hit-stop
5. shockwave
6. follow-through
7. recovery
8. environmental reaction
```

---

# 13. FULL EXAMPLE

## Weak Action Description

```text id="j7x3kv"
The warrior attacks quickly and hits the enemy.
```

---

## Strong Action Description

```text id="1l7o8b"
The warrior lowers his stance slightly.

A brief anticipation pause.

Suddenly, he launches forward with explosive acceleration.

The camera whip-pans violently during the dash.

The strike lands with a heavy hit-stop frame.

A delayed shockwave tears through the battlefield.

Dust and debris erupt outward from the collision.

The warrior slides backward slightly from recoil before regaining balance.
```

---

# 14. CORE PRINCIPLE

Weak action describes:

> movement

Strong action describes:

> force, timing, inertia, and reaction

The audience should FEEL the impact,
not simply SEE the attack.
